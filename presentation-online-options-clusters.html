Beyond Embedded Librarianship?

One option: A consultative approach

Consultative Approach

Views embedded librarianship as just one tool in the toolbox, or as one part of a larger instructional approach or plan.

Consultative Approach

Lead liaison(s) working with a team to design, implement, and assess an instructional plan for their cluster.

  • Leverage a rich set of tools and diverse instructional approaches (examples?)
  • Structure their work as an instructional design process (assessment as continual improvement)
  • Foster foundational research skills and potentially transformative experiences with/in/out the library

What's an Engagement plan?

Like a "Service " in that it is intended to be sustainable, but also tailored for each instructional context.

Likely includes a curated selection of modular instructional elements, as well as some unique to the learning community/context.

Digital Research Notebook

  • offers best direct & authentic evidence of student learning
  • good on its own, better when tailored to an assignment w/ scaffolded process
  • takes time to set up and to analyze, interpret, quantify student learning

Best for: scaffolding, focusing on process, seeing authentic examples of student research skills

CCLE

  • gradebook integration
  • options for both quantitative and qualitative assessment
  • can be tailored to the assignment
  • possibility to participate in IDP grant
  • takes some time and technical expertise to set up

Best for: quantitative assessment/analysis of student activity completion. Also best if instruction team already uses CCLE for many of their activities
